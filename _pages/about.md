This site is actively under construction.  

I am predoctoral fellow in Dr. Andrew Shaver's Political Violence Lab. In the fall I will begin my PhD in Political Violence at Duke University studying Security, Peace, and Conflict. 

I received my M.S. from the University of Utah in Political Science and my B.A. from the University of Arkansas in Middle East Studies/ Arabic. Currently, I am engaging in projects investigating the determinants of forced migration and the effects that refugees have on host communities. I have general substantive interests in political violence, the nexus of substate and international conflict, and the political economy of development. Methodologically I am interested in computational social science and leveraging microlevel data to uncover causal relationships.
